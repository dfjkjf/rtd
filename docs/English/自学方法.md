To improve your English, you need to turn the words you **know** (reading) into words you can **use** (speaking). Here is a simple, combined strategy:

## 1. Stop Learning Words, Start Learning "Chunks"
Native speakers don't build sentences word-by-word like LEGO blocks. We use **lexical chunks**—groups of words that naturally go together.

* **Instead of learning:** "Decision."
* **Learn the chunk:** "To **make** a decision" (we rarely say "do" a decision).
* **Instead of learning:** "Heavy."
* **Learn the chunk:** "**Heavy** rain" or "**Heavy** traffic."

When you learn chunks, your brain doesn't have to work as hard to "build" the sentence; you just grab the whole phrase and go.

## 2. Use the "Shadowing" Technique
This is one of the most effective ways to get the "music" of English into your head. 

1.  Find a clip of a native speaker (a YouTube video, a podcast, or a movie scene).
2.  Listen to 5 seconds of audio.
3.  **Immediately repeat it**, trying to mimic their speed, emotion, and where they "connect" words.
4.  Do this until you can say the sentence at the exact same time as the speaker. 

## 3. Learn "Connectors" (The Glue)
Native speakers use small words to keep the flow going. If you don't use these, your speech sounds like a list of facts.

* **To add info:** "Plus," "Also," "On top of that."
* **To show contrast:** "Mind you," "Then again," "Actually."
* **To buy time (Fillers):** "You know," "I mean," "To be honest."

## 4. Think in English (The "Internal Monologue")
If you translate from your native language, your sentence structure will always feel slightly "off." 

* Try to narrate your day in your head: *"I'm going to grab a coffee now. Should I get a croissant? No, I'll skip it today."*
* If you don't know a word, don't look it up immediately—try to describe it in English instead.

### A Common Pitfall: Formal vs. Natural
Many learners study textbooks and end up saying things like, *"I shall go to the store now."* While grammatically perfect, a native speaker would say, *"I'm gonna head to the shop."*

| Textbook English | Native/Natural English |
| :--- | :--- |
| "I am very tired." | "I'm exhausted" or "I'm wiped out." |
| "I understand you." | "I get what you mean" or "I hear you." |
| "Please wait a moment." | "Just a sec" or "Hang on a bit." |
